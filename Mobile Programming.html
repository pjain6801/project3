Project 1: Commmunication Design (Incyte)

Project Overview

Our client, Incyte, is a global biopharmaceutical that is conducting a clinical trial for treating Vitiligo. Our team created a machine learning algorithm for the client that will use Vitiligo patients' skin photos to provide patients feedback on their condition.

Our team was brought in for 2 major reasons:

1) Create a brochure which will be distributed to Vitiligo patients encouraging them to take pictures of their skin.
2) Create an app for the patients to take those pictures and help them track their condition improvements.

Design Principles

Information Hierarchy:

‍In addition to being provided with informational content for the brochure, I also generated content through doing company research. I thought of this brochure as a information architecture design problem. I structured pieces of information as bullet points and small paragraphs to make it easily consumable to readers and utilized white space to break up the content visually on the page. It was an iterative process to design the information in the most effective way for consumption.

Progressive Disclosure:

The organizational structure of of a brochure fully embraces the importance of progressive disclosure. I took advantage of the brochure format by putting a 'hook' statement on the front page. After the reader opens the pamphlet, their eyes would be drawn to the the numbers written with a pop of purple. The color contrast and surrounding white space will catch the readers attention as they navigate the content.

App Design

I was then tasked to create mid-fidelity screens of a patient-facing app to help patients track improvements in their skin conditions. This app includes features of finding a doctor, chatting with a doctor, and reviewing patient's improvement.

Project 3: Data Dashboard Design

Project Overview

Our client, Genmab, is an international biotechnology company that wanted to create a dashboard interface for all their employees. After speaking to multiple stakeholders, I learned that they wanted a website where all of the software and applications their employees use could be in one place with a single sign-on feature. I also learned that website would have an employee and admin functionality. In the admin side, they would be given control over adding or editing applications, user management and adding updates or announcements.

Design Principles

Information Hierarchy:

‍‍I worked with the design team in the ideation process of designing the screens. I branched out off their designs with slight modifications to present to our client. I created a top navigation bar so the user can easily access all the apps. On the right I made a calendar feature for the user to stay on top of upcoming meetings. The rest of the screen displays snapshots of important data that's relevant to the user.

Consistent Design Identity:

I also made sure to use a consistent design identity for both versions of the interface, but enough contrast so that the admin and employee versions are different.

Project 4: Communication Design (Mobile Programming)

Project Overview

I was tasked to create a multi-page pamphlet about our company. The purpose of the pamphlet was to replace slide decks which were used in meetings with new clients. My goal was to create an overall pamphlet structure that breaks down three things: who we are, what services we provide, and why should potential clients choose us. After the main pamphlet was made, I was then tasked to create additional pamphlets that were centered around our industries of expertise, for example Healthcare, Banking and Finance, and Pharma.

Design Principles

Emphasis:

‍‍I created emphasis by using geometric lines and shapes to draw the user's attention towards information. For example, the first page has a hang reaching up to the clouds, which carry two chunks of content. The color contrast between different parts of the page also helps put emphasis.

Pattern:

I used different spatial forms in the same way to create a pattern. For example, the repeated use of hexagonal icons adds variety to the page, sparking the readers' interest towards that content. I also used the color pattern of grey and blue hroughout the pamphlet.
